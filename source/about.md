I am computer scientist with a strong foundation in computer vision and artificial intelligence, currently working as a Research Associate in the Department of Soil & Crop Sciences at Texas A&M University. For the past five years, my research has focused on developing open-source software solutions that allow small farmers and researchers to extract physiological information from their crops. During this time I became aware of the importance of high-throughput phenotyping, as well as the lack of robust software tools used for analysis. This epiphany created a desire within me to apply computer vision techniques to the field of agriculture.
